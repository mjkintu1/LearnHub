1. Grammar: Parts of Speech

Nouns: Name of a person, place, or thing.
Example: John, Kampala, car

Verbs: Indicate actions or states.
Example: run, is, speak

Adjectives: Describe nouns.
Example: beautiful, fast, tall

Adverbs: Describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.
Example: quickly, very, well

Pronouns, Prepositions, Conjunctions, and Interjections are also key parts with unique roles in sentence construction.

2. Tenses and Their Usage

Simple Present: Describes regular actions.
Example: She eats breakfast at 7 AM.

Past Perfect: Indicates actions completed before another past action.
Example: They had left before I arrived.

Future Continuous: Shows ongoing action in the future.
Example: I will be studying at 8 PM.

3. Sentence Structure

Simple Sentence: Contains one independent clause.
Example: The dog barked.

Compound Sentence: Two independent clauses joined by a conjunction.
Example: She cooked dinner, and he cleaned the house.

Complex Sentence: An independent clause and at least one dependent clause.
Example: Although it was raining, we went outside.

4. Punctuation

Commas: Separate elements in a list or clauses.
Example: I bought apples, oranges, and bananas.

Colons & Semicolons: Introduce lists or link related ideas.
Example: She has three hobbies: reading, writing, and hiking.
I like pizza; however, I prefer pasta.

5. Common Errors in English

Misplaced Modifiers:
Incorrect: She almost drove her kids to school every day.
Correct: She drove her kids to school almost every day.

Subject-Verb Agreement:
Incorrect: The list of items are on the table.
Correct: The list of items is on the table.

6. Figures of Speech

Simile: Comparison using 'like' or 'as'.
Example: As brave as a lion.

Metaphor: Direct comparison.
Example: Time is a thief.

Personification: Giving human traits to non-human things.
Example: The wind whispered through the trees.

7. Writing Skills

Paragraph Writing: Topic sentence, supporting details, and conclusion.

Essay Writing: Includes introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion.

Letter Writing: Formal and informal formats.
